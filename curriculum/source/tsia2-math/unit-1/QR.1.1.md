#### **Part 1: Guided Notes**

##### What Are Rational and Irrational Numbers?

A **rational number** is any number that can be written as a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$, where $a$ and $b$ are integers and $b \neq 0$.

Examples of rational numbers:
- $\frac{1}{2}$ (one-half)
- $\frac{3}{4}$ (three-fourths)
- $5 = \frac{5}{1}$ (five can be written as a fraction)
- $0.75 = \frac{3}{4}$ (decimals that end are rational)
- $0.333... = 0.\overline{3} = \frac{1}{3}$ (repeating decimals are rational)

An **irrational number** is a number that cannot be written as a simple fraction. Its decimal form goes on forever without repeating a pattern.

Examples of irrational numbers:
- $\sqrt{2} \approx 1.41421356...$
- $\pi \approx 3.14159265...$
- $\sqrt{3}, \sqrt{5}, \sqrt{7}$ (square roots of non-perfect squares)
- $e \approx 2.71828...$ (Euler's number - less common on TSIA2, but good to know)

**Key difference:** You *can* write a rational number exactly as a fraction. You *cannot* write an irrational number exactly as a fraction, no matter how hard you try.

---

##### Comparing Rational Numbers

When you need to compare two rational numbers, convert them to the same form (usually decimals) and then compare.

**Example 1:** Which is larger, $\frac{3}{4}$ or $\frac{2}{3}$?

Step 1: Convert both to decimals.
- $\frac{3}{4} = 0.75$
- $\frac{2}{3} = 0.666...$

Step 2: Compare the decimals.
- $0.75 > 0.666...$

Step 3: Write your answer.
- $\frac{3}{4} > \frac{2}{3}$

**Example 2:** Order these from smallest to largest: $\frac{1}{2}, 0.6, \frac{3}{5}$

Step 1: Convert all to decimals.
- $\frac{1}{2} = 0.5$
- $0.6 = 0.6$ (already a decimal)
- $\frac{3}{5} = 0.6$

Step 2: Order them.
- $0.5 < 0.6 = 0.6$

Step 3: Rewrite using original forms.
- $\frac{1}{2} < \frac{3}{5} = 0.6$

(Notice that $\frac{3}{5}$ and $0.6$ are the same number, just written differently.)

---

##### Comparing Irrational Numbers

Irrational numbers (like square roots) are trickier because you can't write them as exact decimals. But you *can* estimate them and then compare.

**Key trick:** Know the perfect squares!
- $\sqrt{1} = 1$
- $\sqrt{4} = 2$
- $\sqrt{9} = 3$
- $\sqrt{16} = 4$
- $\sqrt{25} = 5$
- $\sqrt{36} = 6$
- $\sqrt{100} = 10$

If you see $\sqrt{5}$, ask yourself: "Is 5 between two perfect squares?"
- Yes: $4 < 5 < 9$
- So: $\sqrt{4} < \sqrt{5} < \sqrt{9}$
- Which means: $2 < \sqrt{5} < 3$
- So $\sqrt{5}$ is between 2 and 3, closer to 2. (It's about 2.236.)

**Example 3:** Which is larger, $\sqrt{7}$ or $2.5$?

Step 1: Figure out where $\sqrt{7}$ falls.
- $\sqrt{7}$ is between $\sqrt{4} = 2$ and $\sqrt{9} = 3$
- So $2 < \sqrt{7} < 3$
- More specifically, $\sqrt{7} \approx 2.646$

Step 2: Compare to 2.5.
- $2.646 > 2.5$

Step 3: Write your answer.
- $\sqrt{7} > 2.5$

---

##### Comparing Rational and Irrational Numbers

The strategy is the same: convert irrational numbers to decimals (as estimates), then compare.

**Example 4:** Which is larger, $\frac{5}{2}$ or $\sqrt{6}$?

Step 1: Convert $\frac{5}{2}$ to a decimal.
- $\frac{5}{2} = 2.5$

Step 2: Estimate $\sqrt{6}$.
- $\sqrt{6}$ is between $\sqrt{4} = 2$ and $\sqrt{9} = 3$
- It's closer to $\sqrt{4}$, so it's closer to 2.
- $\sqrt{6} \approx 2.449$

Step 3: Compare.
- $2.5 > 2.449$

Step 4: Write your answer.
- $\frac{5}{2} > \sqrt{6}$

#### **Part 4: Answer Key**

##### Practice Problems - Worked Solutions

**Basic Level**

**1. Which of the following is a rational number?**

A) $\sqrt{2}$ - This is irrational. Its decimal form is $1.41421356...$, non-repeating and non-terminating.

B) $\pi$ - This is irrational. Its decimal form is $3.14159...$, non-repeating and non-terminating.

C) $\frac{7}{2}$ - **This is rational.** It's already written as a fraction where the numerator and denominator are integers.

D) $\sqrt{10}$ - This is irrational. Its decimal form is $3.162277...$, non-repeating and non-terminating.

**Answer: C**

---

**2. Convert to a decimal: $\frac{4}{5}$**

Method: Divide 4 by 5.

$\frac{4}{5} = 4 \div 5 = 0.8$

**Answer: 0.8**

---

**3. Which is larger: $\frac{2}{3}$ or $\frac{3}{5}$? Show your work.**

Step 1: Convert both to decimals.
- $\frac{2}{3} = 0.666...$
- $\frac{3}{5} = 0.6$

Step 2: Compare.
- $0.666... > 0.6$

**Answer: $\frac{2}{3}$ is larger.**

---

**4. Between which two integers does $\sqrt{10}$ fall?**

Step 1: List perfect squares near 10.
- $\sqrt{1} = 1, \sqrt{4} = 2, \sqrt{9} = 3, \sqrt{16} = 4$

Step 2: Find where 10 fits.
- $9 < 10 < 16$

Step 3: Take square roots.
- $\sqrt{9} < \sqrt{10} < \sqrt{16}$
- $3 < \sqrt{10} < 4$

**Answer: $\sqrt{10}$ is between 3 and 4.**

---

**5. Order from smallest to largest: $0.8, \frac{3}{4}, \frac{4}{5}$**

Step 1: Convert all to decimals.
- $0.8 = 0.8$
- $\frac{3}{4} = 0.75$
- $\frac{4}{5} = 0.8$

Step 2: Order them.
- $0.75 < 0.8 = 0.8$

Step 3: Rewrite using original forms.
- $\frac{3}{4} < 0.8 = \frac{4}{5}$

**Answer: $\frac{3}{4}, 0.8, \frac{4}{5}$ (note that $0.8$ and $\frac{4}{5}$ are the same)**

---

**Proficient Level**

**6. Which is larger, $\sqrt{11}$ or $3.3$? Explain your reasoning.**

Step 1: Estimate $\sqrt{11}$.
- Perfect squares near 11: $\sqrt{9} = 3$ and $\sqrt{16} = 4$
- $9 < 11 < 16$, so $3 < \sqrt{11} < 4$
- 11 is between 9 and 16, but closer to 9, so $\sqrt{11}$ is closer to 3.
- Using estimation: $\sqrt{11} \approx 3.3166...$

Step 2: Compare to 3.3.
- $3.3166 > 3.3$

**Answer: $\sqrt{11}$ is larger.**

---

**7. A student claims that $\sqrt{25}$ is irrational. Is the student correct? Why or why not?**

Step 1: Evaluate $\sqrt{25}$.
- $\sqrt{25} = 5$

Step 2: Determine if 5 is rational or irrational.
- $5 = \frac{5}{1}$, which can be written as a fraction.
- So 5 is rational.

Step 3: Conclude.
- $\sqrt{25}$ equals 5, which is rational.

**Answer: No, the student is not correct. $\sqrt{25} = 5$, and 5 is a rational number because it can be written as the fraction $\frac{5}{1}$. The square root symbol alone does not make a number irrational.**

---

**8. Order from smallest to largest: $\frac{5}{6}, \sqrt{1}, 0.99, \frac{11}{12}$**

Step 1: Convert all to decimals.
- $\frac{5}{6} = 0.8333...$
- $\sqrt{1} = 1$
- $0.99 = 0.99$
- $\frac{11}{12} = 0.9166...$

Step 2: Order them.
- $0.8333... < 0.9166... < 0.99 < 1$

Step 3: Rewrite using original forms.
- $\frac{5}{6} < \frac{11}{12} < 0.99 < \sqrt{1}$

**Answer: $\frac{5}{6}, \frac{11}{12}, 0.99, \sqrt{1}$**

---

**9. Which of the following is closest to $2.8$?**

Step 1: Convert all to decimals.
- A) $\frac{8}{3} = 2.666...$
- B) $\frac{14}{5} = 2.8$
- C) $\sqrt{8} \approx 2.828...$
- D) $\sqrt{10} \approx 3.162...$

Step 2: Find distances from 2.8.
- A) $|2.666... - 2.8| = 0.133...$
- B) $|2.8 - 2.8| = 0$
- C) $|2.828... - 2.8| = 0.028...$
- D) $|3.162... - 2.8| = 0.362...$

Step 3: Identify the smallest distance.
- B has distance 0 - it equals 2.8 exactly.

**Answer: B** ($\frac{14}{5} = 2.8$ exactly)

**10. Maria's water bottle holds $1.5$ liters. Juan's holds $\frac{8}{5}$ liters. Whose bottle holds more water?**

Step 1: Convert both to the same form.
- Maria's bottle: $1.5$ liters
- Juan's bottle: $\frac{8}{5} = 1.6$ liters

Step 2: Compare.
- $1.6 > 1.5$

**Answer: Juan's bottle holds more water.**

**Advanced Level**

**11. Compare $\sqrt{15}$ and $\frac{7}{2}$. Which is larger? Justify your answer with specific decimal estimates.**

Step 1: Estimate $\sqrt{15}$.
- Perfect squares near 15: $\sqrt{9} = 3$ and $\sqrt{16} = 4$
- $9 < 15 < 16$, so $3 < \sqrt{15} < 4$
- Since 15 is closer to 16 than to 9, $\sqrt{15}$ is closer to 4.
- More precisely: $\sqrt{15} \approx 3.873...$

Step 2: Convert $\frac{7}{2}$ to a decimal.
- $\frac{7}{2} = 3.5$

Step 3: Compare.
- $3.873... > 3.5$

**Answer: $\sqrt{15}$ is larger. Justification: $\sqrt{15} \approx 3.873$ and $\frac{7}{2} = 3.5$, so $\sqrt{15} > \frac{7}{2}$.**

---

**12. A number is described as: "It's irrational, and it's between 3 and 4." Which of the following could it be?**

Step 1: Check each option.

A) $\frac{10}{3} = 3.333...$
- This is between 3 and 4. ✓
- But it's rational (it's a fraction). ✗

B) $\sqrt{12}$
- Estimate: $\sqrt{9} = 3$ and $\sqrt{16} = 4$, so $3 < \sqrt{12} < 4$. ✓
- $\sqrt{12}$ cannot be simplified to a single integer (it simplifies to $2\sqrt{3}$, which is still irrational). ✓
- This is irrational and between 3 and 4. ✓✓

C) $3.5$
- This is between 3 and 4. ✓
- But $3.5 = \frac{7}{2}$, which is rational. ✗

D) $\sqrt{3.5}$
- $\sqrt{3.5}$ is irrational. ✓
- But watch the trap: the $3.5$ sits *under* the radical, so it is not the value. Take the root and $\sqrt{3.5} \approx 1.871$, which lands between 1 and 2, not between 3 and 4. ✗

Only B satisfies both conditions.

**Answer: B**

##### Mini Quiz - Answer Key

**Item 1: Which of the following is irrational?**

A) $0.333...$ - This equals $\frac{1}{3}$, a rational number.

B) $\sqrt{16}$ - This equals 4, a rational number (whole numbers are rational).

C) $\sqrt{20}$ - This cannot be written as a fraction. Its decimal form is $4.472135...$, non-repeating and non-terminating. **This is irrational.**

D) $\frac{9}{4}$ - This is already a fraction, so it's rational.

**Answer: C**

---

**Item 2: Order from least to greatest: $\frac{3}{5}, 0.65, \frac{5}{8}$**

Step 1: Convert to decimals.
- $\frac{3}{5} = 0.6$
- $0.65 = 0.65$
- $\frac{5}{8} = 0.625$

Step 2: Order them.
- $0.6 < 0.625 < 0.65$

**Answer: B** - $\frac{3}{5}, \frac{5}{8}, 0.65$

---

**Item 3: Which is larger: $\sqrt{8}$ or $2.9$?**

Step 1: Estimate $\sqrt{8}$.
- $\sqrt{4} = 2$ and $\sqrt{9} = 3$, so $2 < \sqrt{8} < 3$
- More precisely: $\sqrt{8} = \sqrt{4 \cdot 2} = 2\sqrt{2} \approx 2.828...$

Step 2: Compare to 2.9.
- $2.828 < 2.9$

**Answer: B** - $2.9$ is larger.

---

**Item 4: A student says: "$\sqrt{4}$ is irrational because it has a square root sign." What is wrong with this thinking?**

The student is confusing the square root *symbol* with irrationality. The square root symbol doesn't make a number irrational - it depends on whether the result can be written as a fraction.

$\sqrt{4} = 2 = \frac{2}{1}$, which is rational.

**Answer: A** - $\sqrt{4}$ is rational because $\sqrt{4} = 2$, which can be written as a fraction.
